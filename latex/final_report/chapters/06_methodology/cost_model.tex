\section{Cost and Dynamic Batching Models}
\label{sec:cost_models}
The theoretical dynamic batching cost model described in early literature (aimed at algorithmically predicting optimal batch sizes based on fluctuating L1 base fees) is analytical and is not fully implemented in the active runtime codebase. For the empirical benchmarking, static configurations are swept explicitly to gather data on cost behavior.
\begin{table}[h]
\centering
\caption{ZK-Rollup Cost Model \cite{chaliasos2024analyzing, saif2024dataavailability}}
\begin{tabular}{|l|l|l|l|}
\hline
\textbf{Cost Category} & \textbf{Examples} & \textbf{Fixed/Marginal} & \textbf{Source} \\ \hline
L1 Settlement & Commit batch, verify proof & Fixed per batch & Chaliasos \cite{chaliasos2024analyzing} \\ \hline
Proof Compression & STARK-to-SNARK / aggregation & Fixed or batch-level & Chaliasos \cite{chaliasos2024analyzing} \\ \hline
Data Availability (DA) & calldata/state diff/blob bytes & Marginal / payload-dependent & Chaliasos, Saif \cite{chaliasos2024analyzing, saif2024dataavailability} \\ \hline
Proving & witness + proof generation & Marginal and batch-dependent & Chaliasos \cite{chaliasos2024analyzing} \\ \hline
L2 Execution & sequencing, execution, storage & Marginal / system cost & Chaliasos \cite{chaliasos2024analyzing} \\ \hline
\end{tabular}
\end{table}

The cost model splits into fixed batch costs (L1 settlement, proof verification) and marginal transaction costs (DA bytes, witness generation, execution). Larger batches reduce the amortized fixed cost per transaction but increase latency \cite{chaliasos2024analyzing}.
$$ \text{Cost per transaction} = \left(\frac{\text{fixed batch settlement/proof cost}}{\text{batch size}}\right) + \text{marginal DA/proving/execution cost per transaction} $$
