\section{Evaluation Metrics}
\label{sec:evaluation_metrics}
Telemetry data is collected as raw JSON/CSV metrics in the \texttt{benchmark-suite/metrics/} directory. The Data Tools analytics pipeline subsequently processes this data using Python aggregation scripts to compute the final metrics.

\begin{table}[H]
\centering
\caption{Key Benchmarking Metrics}
\label{tab:metrics}
\small
\begin{tabularx}{\textwidth}{l X c X}
\toprule
\textbf{Metric} & \textbf{Definition} & \textbf{Unit} & \textbf{Why it matters} \\
\midrule
Throughput & Rate of successful transaction inclusion & TPS & Indicates capacity limits of the system \\
Latency & Time from transaction submission to soft finality & ms & Affects user experience \\
Finalization & Time from batch sealing to hard finality on L1 & ms & Affects system security and dispute windows \\
Gas Cost & L1 execution cost per transaction or per batch & Wei & Determines the economic viability of the rollup \\
DA Footprint & Size of the compressed data posted to L1 & Bytes & Directly correlates with calldata costs \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabularx}
\end{table}
Key metrics include Committed TPS (transactions committed to L1 per second), average and P95 latency, proof delay (seconds per proof), DA bytes posted, cost per transaction, and batch finalization time \cite{chaliasos2024analyzing}.

We rigorously define several metric stages: Submitted TPS, Accepted TPS, Batched TPS, Committed TPS, and Verified TPS. Average queueing latency, P95/P99 latency, and DA bytes per transaction are also heavily tracked.
