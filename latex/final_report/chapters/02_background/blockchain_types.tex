\subsection{Blockchain Types}
While this project focuses on public, permissionless blockchains (specifically Ethereum), blockchains can also be permissioned or consortium-based. Public blockchains prioritize broad, decentralized participation at the cost of performance, whereas permissioned systems (e.g., Hyperledger Fabric) restrict access to known entities, allowing for more efficient, high-throughput consensus mechanisms like PBFT or Raft. However, for decentralized financial applications, the security guarantees of a public L1 like Ethereum are typically strictly required.
