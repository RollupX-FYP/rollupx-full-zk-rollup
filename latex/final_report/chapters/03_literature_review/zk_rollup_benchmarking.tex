\section{Rollup Benchmarking and Public Datasets}
\label{sec:rollup_benchmarking}

Several recent studies analyze public datasets from deployed systems. For example, Silva et al. \cite{silva2025zksyncdataset} provide comprehensive analyses of zkSync Era traces. These studies offer valuable, empirical insight into real-world rollup behavior. 

However, because they primarily observe existing, live deployments, they are less suited for isolating the causal impact of individual configuration choices. In a production environment, it is practically impossible for a researcher to independently modify the sequencer's internal batching rules, scheduling algorithms, or DA modes under strictly identical, controlled workloads to observe the isolated effects.
Benchmarking ZK-rollups is multifaceted: deployment is complex, design choices differ, and metrics are non-trivial. Chaliasos et al. state that common payloads are hard to define due to varying compatibility levels across ZK-EVMs \cite{chaliasos2024analyzing}.
