\subsection{RQ1: Modular Benchmarking Design}
The prototype successfully demonstrates that a modular architecture—separating the Workload Generator, Sequencer, Executor, Prover, and Submitter—is highly effective for controlled benchmarking. Unlike monolithic production systems, this separation allows researchers to isolate specific variables (e.g., swapping DA modes in the Submitter without altering the Sequencer's batching logic). The use of containerized microservices and TOML/YAML configuration templates proved essential for executing automated, reproducible configuration sweeps.
